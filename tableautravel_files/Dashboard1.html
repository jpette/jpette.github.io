In accordance with the Mutual Security Act of 1954 (Title 22 U.S. Code, Chapter 24, Section 1754) and the International Security Assistance Act of 1978, Members of the U.S. Senate and House of Representatives must publish reports of certain expenditures for all official foreign travel.

The visualization tool below illustrates these expenditures from 2007 to the present and allows for some filtering of parameters of interest including: Years of travel, Political Party, Destination, and Traveler. The information can be examined through multiple measures of the expenditures: mean trip spend amount, median trip spend amount, total spend, maximum trip spend (i.e most expensive trip), and number of trips.
